The Impeachment Process: A Brief Overview

At its core, impeachment is a formal process by which Congress holds elected officials accountable for any number of high crimes and misdemeanors committed while in office. The Constitution grants Congress broad authority over the impeachment process, but it also sets clear parameters around what constitutes grounds for removal from office.

In order for impeachment proceedings to begin, a simple majority vote in either house of Congress must first occur. Once an official is formally impeached, they are tried before the Senate, where a two-thirds majority vote is required for conviction. If convicted, the official is removed from office and may face criminal charges as well.
