Impeachment, the constitutional remedy for addressing high crimes and misdemeanors committed by public officials, has long been a contentious issue in American politics. The Impeachment Process

At its core, impeachment is a constitutionally mandated process through which Congress exercises its authority over the executive branch. The House of Representatives initiates impeachment proceedings by voting on articles of impeachment, which outline specific abuses of power or other misconduct alleged against the targeted official. If a simple majority votes in favor of the articles, the case is then sent to the Senate for a trial, where a two-thirds majority vote is required to convict and remove the official from office.
